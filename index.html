Welcome

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the Department of Computer Science of the University of Surrey, in the United Kingdom.

Academic adventure

I did my undergraduate studies in Mathematics at the University of Antananarivo. In 2010, I moved to South Africa to pursue the AIMS programme at the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences. I got the opportunity to continue my study with a Master and a PhD (2016) in South Africa at the University of Stellenbosh.

In 2016, I moved to Germany where I was taking care of my family as my wife worked at the University of Bonn. During that time, I was a part-time research visitor at the MOVES group of the University of Aachen. In 2017, we left Germany and went back to Madagascar. Between 2018 and 2019 I worked a contractual lecturer at the Ecole Normale Superieure of Antananarivo. We moved again in 2020 when I got my current postdoc position at the University of Surrey, in the UK.(to be continued)

Research interests

My current areas of research include

  • Security Protocols (formal analysis of privacy in security protocols)
  • Multi-agent Epistemic Logics (modelling agents knowledge, knowledge dynamics, strategy, ...)
  • Formal Methods in Computer Science (model checking, formal program refinement, ...)
