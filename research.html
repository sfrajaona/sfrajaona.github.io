Research Interests

  • Analysis of privacy in cryptographic protocols
  • Multi-agent Epistemic Logics (modelling agents knowledge, knowledge dynamics, strategy, ...)
  • Formal Methods in Computer Science (model checking, formal program refinement, ...)
  • Development of Model Checkers (mainly Haskell and a little bit of Python)
  • Visual Techniques for Solving Logical Puzzles

Current Research Activities

In no particular order

  • Expressing and verifying sessions unlinkability properties as a trace property (Tool Development, Theoretical Work).

  • Case studies AKMA protocol with the Chloe Model Checker FM2023 and with SQUIRELL Prover (Practical Case Study)

  • Hidden/Atomic Actions to the framework in FM2023 (Theoretical Work)

  • Analysing voting protocol with Phoebe Model Checker CSF2024 (Practical Case Study)

  • Use of phoebe property specifications and model checking techniques in a less crypto application, e.g., medical software

  • Adding Loops to FM2023 (Theoretical and Tool development)

  • Programming techniques for Probabilistic Systems & Agents with Imperfect Knowledge with applications to Quantitative Information Flow and (maybe) Differential Privacy (Theoretical work). This is an ambitious project. The idea is to use the approach in FM2023 with probabilistic programs (Morgan & McIver PGCL).
