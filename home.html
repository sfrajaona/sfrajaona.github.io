Welcome

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the Department of Computer Science of the University of Surrey, in the United Kingdom.

Academic adventure

When I started my undergraduate studies in Mathematics at the University of Antananarivo (2006), I didn't know what sort of career to expect. Maths was an unpopular subject at that time. Most students end up doing it only when they fail at other subjects, or when they run out of choices. Now, more and more students study maths to later pursue other IT studies. In 2006, I chose math as my first option, like very few of us during that time. I did maths because sometimes I like it. In school, I liked plane geometry, probability, math puzzles, and math jokes! I hated quadratic equations, factorisations etc.

Despite having no clear direction for my career (and other unseriousnesses), I considered myself very fortunate to get my Diplome de Maîtrise en Mathematiques in 2009 with mention bien. A few months later I was selected by the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS) to pursue the AIMS programme. At AIMS, we had great lecturers like Tadashi Tokieda and Alan Beardon, who taught very exciting Mathematics. I stayed at AIMS for five more years, doing my MSc and my PhD under the supervision of Jeff Sanders. During that time, I married my girlfriend, whom I already dated in Madagascar. Again, I was very fortunate that both my girlfriend and I got selected by AIMS.

In 2016, I moved to Germany to look after my family while my wife was working at the University of Bonn. I was also a part-time research visitor at the MOVES group of the University of Aachen. Neither my career nor my wife's career took off in Germany, so we went back to Madagascar in July 2017. We lived adventurously in Fort-Dauphin during the second half of 2017, but could not start a career there, so we moved back to Antananarivo. There, I worked as a contractual lecturer at the Ecole Normale Superieure between 2018-2019. It was a great teaching experience with brilliant students, but the work conditions were hard for a contractual lecturer (vacataire), so I looked elsewhere. In 2020, I got my current postdoc position at the University of Surrey. (to be continued)

Research interests

My current areas of research include

  • Security Protocols (formal analysis of privacy in security protocols)
  • Multi-agent Epistemic Logics (modelling agents knowledge, knowledge dynamics, strategy, ...)
  • Formal Methods in Computer Science (model checking, formal program refinement, ...)

Other interests

I'm interested in Maths and Science Education in Madagascar.
