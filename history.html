Fortunat Rajaona
Pejy Malagasy

I grew up in Madagascar. I studied Mathematics at the University of Antananarivo

I studied at the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences AIMS , South Africa

I got married to Tsinampoizina Marie Sophie Randriamahefasoa in 2012.

After that, I pursued with research studies in Computer Science Formal Methods under the mentorship of Prof. Jeff Sanders. I graduated with an MSc in 2013 at the University of Stellenbosch, South Africa.

In 2013, I was awarded an annual DAAD scholarship to pursue a PhD at the University of Stellenbosch. I graduated in 2016, then I moved to Germany as my wife was doing her research at the University of Bonn.

I was a part-time visiting researcher at the MOVES group of Joost Pieter Katoen at RWTH Aachen University. After that, we went back in Madagascar. I assisted Prof. Charles Ratsifaritana with the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) Madagascar project and worked a contractual lecturer at the École Normale Superieure of the University of Antananarivo from 2018 to 2020.
